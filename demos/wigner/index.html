The Wigner function is the closest thing to a "probability density" in phase space (x = position quadrature, p = momentum quadrature). Classical-ish states stay positive; negative regions (the blue fringes of cat and Fock states) are a smoking-gun signature of genuinely nonclassical light. Watch the negativity volume and the uncertainty product ΔxΔp ≥ ½.

Reading the plot

Warm = positive, cool/blue = negative (impossible classically). A coherent state is a fuzzy ball at (⟨x⟩,⟨p⟩); squeezing flattens it below the vacuum limit along one axis; a cat shows two lobes with interference fringes between them.
